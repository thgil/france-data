Where France drinks

49,385 bars, pubs, and cafés — every débit de boissons registered in France, placed at its real address.

2026-04-19 4 min read

France has 49,385 active débits de boissons — bars, pubs, brasseries, cafés that serve alcohol. Each one is registered in the national SIRENE business directory with a NAF code (56.30Z) and a geocoded address. The map below shows all of them.

A dense north, a bright coastal rim

Paris has 3,120 bars — the most of any single département. But the Nord (1,961) and Pas-de-Calais (1,265) together top it with 3,226. Bretagne is everywhere: Finistère (1,014), Morbihan (931), Ille-et-Vilaine (914), and Côtes-d'Armor together field over 3,000. The Mediterranean coast — Bouches-du-Rhône (1,632), Var (1,042), Hérault (1,013), Alpes-Maritimes (1,004) — runs hot too. The map shows this as a bright coastal rim and a dense northern band, with the familiar diagonale du vide running through the middle.

Most bars (by département)

Fewest bars (by département)

Raw counts favour big départements. Per capita, the picture shifts — small, rural, tourism-heavy départements rise. Finistère, with under a million residents and over 1,000 bars, clearly punches above its weight. The same goes for the Var and Hérault: summer tourism translates directly into licences, and those licences stay registered year-round. The empty middle of France — Haute-Marne (90 bars), Territoire de Belfort (65) — reflects both sparse population and the slow retreat of the village café over the last generation.

The names are a cultural document

59% of bars have a registered business name — the rest trade under the owner's name or a legal entity code. The named ones are a cultural document all on their own. #BRUT. 0% ALCOHOL FREE EXPERIENCE. 100 PRESSIONS. LE TWENTY FOUR SEVEN. A bar called % exists. Hover over the map to see them.

Each dot is a counter, a licence, a 3 AM closing time. The French café isn't just a cultural symbol — it's a regulated, mapped, countable piece of infrastructure. Like pharmacies. Like bakeries. Like doctors. The grid is everywhere. You just have to look.

A note on the data. Bar locations come from INSEE's SIRENE business registry (active establishments with NAF code 56.30Z — débits de boissons), geocoded by INSEE's statistical geolocation file, both as a March 2026 snapshot. Of registered active bars in SIRENE nationally, 49,385 had valid geocoded coordinates. The bars.json source file is 3.8 MB. This story uses the same data pipeline as Story 3 (baguettes).
