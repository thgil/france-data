7,378 baguettes

Every bakery in Île-de-France, placed at its real address. A love letter to French bread, in dots.

2026-04-18 3 min read

France has roughly 35,000 bakeries. Île-de-France alone has 7,378 active boulangeries registered in the national SIRENE business registry — one for every 1,600 residents. The map below shows each one as a single gold dot at its real geocoded address. Watch the city fill up with bread.

Density tracks population, but not perfectly

Paris's 18e arrondissement has 155 bakeries for 202,685 residents. The 8e has 61 for 41,280 — similar per-capita rates, very different feel on the map. The outer communes tell a different story entirely: many villages in Seine-et-Marne have 1 or 2 bakeries serving thousands of residents, and 607 of IDF's 1,300 communes have none recorded. The "baguette desert" mirrors the medical desert: the same ring of low-density outer suburbs that struggles for pharmacies struggles for bread.

The clustering is almost identical — pharmacies and bakeries share the same commercial streets because they answer to the same pressures: foot traffic, independent ownership, neighbourhood scale. The regulated network and the artisanal one ended up building the same geography by different means.

Each dot is a person who wakes up at 3 AM to make dough. A regulated trade — French law ties bakery labelling to on-premises production; a shop that bakes frozen dough can't legally call itself a boulangerie. A local business: French bakeries are overwhelmingly owner-operated, typically within a few minutes' walk of half their customers. A walk-to destination, invisible unless you map it. Then it's everywhere.

A note on the data. Bakery locations come from INSEE's SIRENE business registry (active establishments with NAF code 10.71C — boulangerie et boulangerie-pâtisserie artisanale), geocoded by INSEE's statistical geolocation file, both snapshot March 2026. Of 7,654 active IDF bakeries in SIRENE, 7,378 had valid geocoded coordinates (96.4%). Pharmacy points in the overlay come from the FINESS dataset used in Story 1.

Density per 10,000 residents uses 2010 census population. Communes under 1,000 residents are excluded from the density ranking to avoid small-sample artefacts. The 607 communes with zero recorded bakeries may include very small villages served by nearby towns, or where the BPE count is zero due to classification differences.
